PHASE 1 / MISSION

Know the goal before you receive

① TRACK ② SYNC ③ DEMOD

Goal: receive the satellite signal

ENIGMA-1 is an Earth-observation cubesat that continuously beacons an AX.25 image over 433.500 MHz. Our job is to receive this downlink and recover the image inside. We never transmit: pure passive intercept.

① Antenna tracking (TRACK)

The satellite crosses low orbit fast. The ground antenna must follow its azimuth / elevation in real time or the signal is lost. GPredict computes the orbit from the TLE and drives the rotator.

② RF sync (SYNC)

Match the receiver to the satellite's RF: 433.5 MHz center, Doppler correction, 9600 baud GFSK. Any mismatch and demodulation fails. Confirm these values in the Virtual Antenna.

③ Demodulate (DEMOD)

Feed the received IQ into a GNU Radio flowgraph: FSK demod → AX.25 deframe (G3RUH descramble) → frame reassembly. The original image comes back to life.
