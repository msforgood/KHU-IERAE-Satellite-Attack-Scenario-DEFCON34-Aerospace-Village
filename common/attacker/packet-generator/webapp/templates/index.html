① The target

DEMOSAT is a small satellite that stays alive by keeping its solar panels pointed at the sun. Its attitude is held steady by reaction wheels.

② Your attack

You will send adcs_torque — a legitimate reaction-wheel command — but with an abused value (999 mNm). The wheels overspin, the satellite tumbles, the panels lose the sun, and power generation collapses. A legitimate command becomes an attack when it is abused.

③ How the command reaches the satellite — RF

The satellite is in orbit — you can't plug a cable into it. The only way to reach it is over the air by radio (RF, radio-frequency): the ground station transmits your command as radio waves on the uplink, and the satellite's receiver decodes them. So your text command has to become an actual radio signal first.

④ Turning the signal into an IQ file

We transmit that RF with a software-defined radio (SDR), which doesn't take a command — it plays back a digitized waveform. An IQ file is exactly that: the radio signal stored as a stream of I/Q samples the SDR sends over the air. So you'll assemble the command, then GENERATE turns it into the IQ file you uplink through the VSA.
